Offering Overview

Scale, land control, and corridor identity in the heart of Savannah.

5501 Abercorn Street is a professional-grade sale opportunity positioned for an owner-user, medical-adjacent operator, service-retail concept, or value-add investor seeking meaningful square footage in an established Savannah commercial corridor.

The property combines approximately +-48,943 SF of existing commercial improvements with a +-3.6 acre site. That combination gives a buyer more than a building, it provides parking, circulation, subdivision flexibility, and a platform for phased repositioning.

The core investment argument is replacement cost. At a $5,000,000 asking price, the asset presents a basis near $102 per square foot before buyer diligence adjustments. Replicating comparable improvements, site control, parking, approvals, and delivery timing along Abercorn Street would likely require substantially more capital and time than acquiring the existing asset.

Opportunity

Four credible value-creation paths.

The asset works because the basis, land area, corridor identity, and surrounding demand generators give a buyer optionality. Each path should be tested through zoning, building condition, parking, leasing, and capital planning diligence.

Owner-User Flagship

A business can buy a recognizable Savannah address and control its occupancy cost. The site provides room for operations, parking, branding, and future flexibility. This path fits medical, education, nonprofit, professional service, and regional headquarters users.

Multi-Tenant Reposition

An investor can modernize the building, subdivide space, and target professional office, medical-adjacent, service retail, and showroom users. The surrounding anchors provide a practical tenant story. Value is created through leasing, design, and operational execution.

Medical and Education Adjacent

The nearby hospital network and Savannah Technical College create a clear demand logic. Users tied to healthcare, training, wellness, administration, and outpatient services can benefit from corridor access. This path should be evaluated with parking, code, and use approvals.

Long-Range Redevelopment

The +-3.6 acre site offers a land-control thesis beyond the existing improvements. A buyer can operate or lease the asset while studying future redevelopment alternatives. Any redevelopment path remains subject to zoning, access, site planning, infrastructure, and municipal approval risk.
